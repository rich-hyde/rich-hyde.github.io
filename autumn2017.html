rehearsals

Generally, much better experience - maybe because the work is quite well known among choirs. Still, we had to learn a bit of German pronunciation.

Mega reverb in the CCS side hall still exacerbating the problems in rehearsals.

Two joint rehearsals with Danesborough chorus at Aspley Guise village hall (decent acoustics)

the performance

Milton Keynes Theatre

Wow! this really came together under the firm baton of Ian Smith. The orchestra was a revelation - didn't realize we had such good musicianship in MK!The soloists were very good and the choir (combined) actually sounded very good too.

This felt really special to be part of and I'm glad to have the memory of this performance to take forward.

The orchestra actually applauded the choir at the end too (that's rare)! Our German pronunciation was very good according to a friend in the audience. It is reported that some on the audience actually wept during the requiem (for the right reasons of course). I'm just sad that it has come and gone in one evening, I'd love to do it again and again.
