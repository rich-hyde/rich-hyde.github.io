Chorale Reflections

This is a set of personal reflections on the last two seasons singing with my local amateur choir - the MK Chorale.

In spite of some specific personal moans (below) this choir can and has given some marvelous performances and produced some excellent sounds. See the concert list below. It's amazing how it can all come together for the big day of the concert after a series of typically pathetic rehearsals! (see also the list of rehearsal and performance venues)

Moans

  • Yes, OK, call me Mr. Negative but: Is this a choir that has a social aspect - or is it an old peoples social club that sometimes sings? They really should make their mind up!

    As a reasonably strong baritone, I find myself standing among a set of bass singers who are around 20 years my senior. I have come realize that I am the only bass in this choir who reads music (well), sings in tune, listens to the music director and practices at home. There have been many times where I've found myself effectively singing a solo because the rest of my section have missed an entry or simply don't know what to sing. This experience extends to actual performances too!

    The music director agrees with me but I don't see anything being addressed. I have offered to get together with some of the basses to go over parts and help with some simple theory - but no takers as yet. He also says "sing it loud and wrong - I can't correct you if I can;t hear you". While I understand and agree with this, it seems the basses, in particular, have extended the practice to performances too - arrgh!

  • Sopranos and Altos (but especially sopranos) tuning!

    Apart from a few, most of the women in the choir seem to think that 'nearly in tune' is good enough. In some a-cappella pieces, the women, on their own, are capable of flattening the pitch by about a semitone per minute! As painful as this is - it is even more annoying for basses who end up having to sing even deeper notes (as a baritone as opposed to a full bass, I particularly feel this one).

    Having listened for a bit longer I now realise that it is just one or two of them that drag the rest down (and the rest of them know who they are - so? ho hum)

    What is it with our altos? There are about 30 of them yet at full blast they only make the sound of one decent singer!

  • Turn up the reverb for rehearsals!

    Our rehearsal venue has such atrocious acoustics that it is nigh on impossible to hear anything once everyone starts to sing (maybe this is how some of them keep getting away with rubbish singing?). Imagine trying to concentrate on your notes, tone and pitching at a crowded indoor swimming pool and you get the picture.
